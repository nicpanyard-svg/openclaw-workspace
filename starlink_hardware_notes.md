# Starlink Hardware Notes for iNet

## Purpose
Quick internal summary of the Starlink kits Nick shared, focused on sales positioning.

## Performance Kit
Best for:
- harsh environments
- remote industrial sites
- critical backup connectivity
- fixed or rugged deployments

Key points:
- download speeds up to 400+ Mbps
- roadmap note says gigabit-enabling network enhancements expected starting in 2026 with service plan upgrades
- survivable wind: 280 kph+ / 174 mph+
- operating temp: -40C to 60C
- snow melt: up to 85 mm/hour
- environmental rating: IP69K with cables, IP68 without cables
- average power: 75-100W
- advanced power supply supports AC and DC, backup battery input, rack mounting, diagnostics, PoE, LAN
- designed for 10-year mission life

Best sales angle:
- utility, midstream, mining, field operations, emergency response, rugged sites
- ideal where resilience and durability matter more than lowest hardware cost

## Standard Kit
Best for:
- mainstream fixed-site deployments
- branch / office / temporary site connectivity
- less harsh outdoor environments

Key points:
- operational wind: 96 kph+ / 60 mph+
- operating temp: -30C to 50C
- snow melt: up to 40 mm/hour
- environmental rating: IP67 Type 4
- average power: 75-100W

Best sales angle:
- solid default option for general business or site connectivity where ruggedization requirements are moderate

## Mini Kit
Best for:
- portable deployments
- mobile users
- fast setup / lightweight use cases
- low power environments

Key points:
- integrated WiFi
- average power: 25-40W
- input: 12-48V
- operational wind: 96 kph+ / 60 mph+
- operating temp: -30C to 50C
- snow melt: up to 25 mm/hour
- one Ethernet LAN port
- WiFi 5 dual-band
- coverage up to 112 m2 / 1,200 sq ft
- up to 128 devices

Best sales angle:
- rapid deployment, temporary jobsites, mobile command, light field use, portable backup

## Simple positioning by market

### Utilities
- Performance Kit for substations, outage response, rugged remote assets
- Standard Kit for general site backup or temporary operations
- Mini for field teams or portable emergency use

### Midstream
- Performance Kit for compressor stations, pump stations, remote monitoring points
- Standard Kit for less extreme fixed sites
- Mini for mobile crews and temporary deployments

### SLED / emergency response
- Performance Kit for command-critical, harsh, disaster-prone environments
- Mini for portable rapid deployment
- Standard for municipal / school / branch fallback use

### Solar / renewables
- Performance Kit where site conditions are harsh and uptime matters
- Standard for construction trailers, O&M buildings, less rugged fixed sites
- Mini for mobile technicians or temporary test deployments

## Sales shorthand
- Performance = rugged, industrial, resilient
- Standard = practical default
- Mini = portable and low power
