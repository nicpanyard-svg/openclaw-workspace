# Graham Stock Board — Initial Candidate List

These are **research candidates for further underwriting**, not final recommendations.

## Selection lens
- public companies still in an earlier scale-up phase
- big category upside if execution continues
- enough liquidity/visibility to monitor properly
- real catalyst path, not just a story

---

## Theme 1: AI infrastructure / compute / data platforms

### 1) ASTS — AST SpaceMobile
- **Why it fits:** very early public-company story, huge TAM if direct-to-device satellite works at scale
- **Watch for:** carrier deals, satellite deployment progress, financing needs
- **Why upside could be large:** category-creator if execution keeps proving out
- **Main risk:** capital intensity and execution delays

### 2) IONQ — IonQ
- **Why it fits:** still early, high-volatility frontier name tied to quantum compute adoption
- **Watch for:** commercial bookings, government work, technical milestones
- **Why upside could be large:** if quantum revenue becomes more real than theoretical
- **Main risk:** long timeline and hype premium

### 3) SOUN — SoundHound AI
- **Why it fits:** smaller AI application-layer name with automotive/enterprise voice exposure
- **Watch for:** customer concentration, margin path, revenue durability
- **Why upside could be large:** if voice AI becomes embedded workflow infrastructure
- **Main risk:** valuation can outrun fundamentals fast

### 4) BBAI — BigBear.ai
- **Why it fits:** emerging AI/decision-intelligence name with defense/government angle
- **Watch for:** contract quality, backlog conversion, profitability path
- **Why upside could be large:** if government AI demand turns into durable program wins
- **Main risk:** execution credibility and lumpy contracts

---

## Theme 2: Defense / autonomy / space

### 5) PL — Planet Labs
- **Why it fits:** earth observation platform with strategic data angle and still-early scale story
- **Watch for:** government uptake, subscription quality, margin improvement
- **Why upside could be large:** data layer for defense, agriculture, climate, logistics
- **Main risk:** slow monetization versus investor expectations

### 6) RKLB — Rocket Lab
- **Why it fits:** one of the cleaner emerging space platforms with launch + space systems exposure
- **Watch for:** Neutron progress, backlog quality, execution cadence
- **Why upside could be large:** can graduate from launch story to full space infrastructure company
- **Main risk:** execution setbacks and capital demands

### 7) AVAV — AeroVironment
- **Why it fits:** more mature than some on this list, but still strong autonomy/defense growth exposure
- **Watch for:** defense budget flows, backlog conversion, integration execution
- **Why upside could be large:** drones/autonomy remains a durable defense trend
- **Main risk:** already better-known, so upside multiple may be less explosive

---

## Theme 3: Digital health / medtech platforms

### 8) HIMS — Hims & Hers Health
- **Why it fits:** consumer health platform still building toward larger scale and broader service mix
- **Watch for:** retention, regulatory issues, margin quality, GLP-1 economics
- **Why upside could be large:** platform expansion beyond a narrow telehealth niche
- **Main risk:** regulatory / reimbursement / competition pressure

### 9) TEM — Tempus AI
- **Why it fits:** young public name with AI + diagnostics + clinical data angle
- **Watch for:** revenue mix, clinical adoption, operating leverage, data moat proof
- **Why upside could be large:** if it becomes a core intelligence layer in precision medicine
- **Main risk:** still proving durability as a public company

### 10) RXRX — Recursion Pharmaceuticals
- **Why it fits:** earlier-stage AI drug discovery platform with asymmetric narrative upside
- **Watch for:** partnership economics, pipeline milestones, cash runway
- **Why upside could be large:** platform value can rerate sharply if evidence improves
- **Main risk:** biotech timelines and binary outcomes

---

## Theme 4: Fintech / market infrastructure / digital platforms

### 11) AFRM — Affirm
- **Why it fits:** still controversial enough to have upside if unit economics and credit performance improve
- **Watch for:** funding costs, delinquency trends, merchant adoption, GAAP profitability
- **Why upside could be large:** if BNPL becomes a durable high-quality network rather than a credit fad
- **Main risk:** macro sensitivity and credit-cycle damage

### 12) DUOL — Duolingo
- **Why it fits:** not tiny, but still feels early in monetization depth and product expansion
- **Watch for:** subscriber growth, AI feature monetization, margin durability
- **Why upside could be large:** strong consumer brand with platform optionality
- **Main risk:** premium valuation leaves little room for misses

### 13) HOOD — Robinhood
- **Why it fits:** platform still expanding beyond trading into wallet, retirement, and broader financial stack
- **Watch for:** asset growth, product breadth, regulatory shifts, retail activity cycles
- **Why upside could be large:** if it matures into a broader financial operating system for retail investors
- **Main risk:** sentiment and trading-volume cyclicality

---

## Theme 5: Industrial automation / robotics / advanced manufacturing

### 14) SERV — Serve Robotics
- **Why it fits:** tiny, early, and highly speculative autonomy/logistics name
- **Watch for:** delivery scale, economics per route, strategic partnerships
- **Why upside could be large:** if autonomous last-mile delivery reaches credible commercial density
- **Main risk:** extreme execution and capital risk

### 15) SYM — Symbotic
- **Why it fits:** warehouse automation platform with real enterprise relevance and strong thematic tailwind
- **Watch for:** deployment quality, customer concentration, margin ramp
- **Why upside could be large:** automation spend could compound for years if execution stays strong
- **Main risk:** lumpy rollouts and dependence on large customers

### 16) PONY — Pony AI
- **Why it fits:** emerging autonomous mobility/public-markets story with large optionality
- **Watch for:** regulation, deployment economics, commercialization progress
- **Why upside could be large:** robotaxi/autonomy winners can rerate hard if adoption becomes real
- **Main risk:** policy and commercialization uncertainty

---

## Theme 6: Energy transition / grid / power electronics

### 17) FLNC — Fluence Energy
- **Why it fits:** grid storage and software exposure in a market that could still be early
- **Watch for:** margins, project execution, order quality, policy support
- **Why upside could be large:** storage demand can scale hard if economics and grid pressure keep improving
- **Main risk:** project volatility and margin disappointments

### 18) NXT — Nextracker
- **Why it fits:** more proven than many names here, but still has room if solar + grid modernization stays strong
- **Watch for:** backlog strength, margin resilience, utility-scale demand
- **Why upside could be large:** can keep compounding if it becomes the standard in its niche
- **Main risk:** less "young" than others; may belong in a steadier-growth bucket over time

---

## Theme 7: Next-gen software / security / developer infrastructure

### 19) CRWD — CrowdStrike
- **Why it fits:** not young in IPO age, but still earlier in platform breadth than its total opportunity suggests
- **Watch for:** platform adoption, margin durability, competitive pressure
- **Why upside could be large:** security platform winners can keep taking share for years
- **Main risk:** likely too mature for the pure emerging board if we want only smaller names

### 20) NET — Cloudflare
- **Why it fits:** edge, security, developer, and AI inference exposure in one platform
- **Watch for:** enterprise ramp, AI monetization, margin trajectory
- **Why upside could be large:** broad infrastructure layer with multiple expansion paths
- **Main risk:** premium valuation and crowded expectations
